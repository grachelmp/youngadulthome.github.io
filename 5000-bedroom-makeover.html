Why $170 is Actually Enough

Most people overspend on their bedroom because they try to do everything at once. New bed frame, new dresser, new everything. That's a recipe for either credit card debt or giving up halfway through.

The secret is focusing on the three things that actually change how a room feels:

1

Lighting

Nothing transforms a room faster than warm, soft lighting. Swap harsh overhead lights for a warm lamp and your room instantly feels cozier and more intentional.

2

Bedding

Your bed takes up 60% of your bedroom visually. Fresh, matching bedding makes the entire room look put-together — even if nothing else has changed.

3

One Statement Piece

One intentional item — a plant, a side table, a diffuser — gives the room a personality. Without it, even a clean room can feel generic and temporary.

How to Actually Do the Makeover

Once your products arrive, here's the order to set everything up for maximum impact:

1

Declutter first

Before anything new goes in, clear off your desk and nightstand area. Toss or box up anything you haven't touched in two weeks. A clean space makes everything else look better automatically.

2

Put on fresh bedding

Make your bed properly — fitted sheet first, then flat sheet, then pillows. Add the throw pillows in front. Step back and look. You'll be surprised how much this alone changes the whole room.

3

Set up the side table and lamp

Place the side table beside your bed. Set the lamp on top. Turn it on, then turn off your overhead light. This is your room's new default mode for evenings.

4

Add your plant

Place it somewhere with indirect sunlight — a desk, windowsill, or shelf. Don't put it somewhere just because it looks good if it won't actually get enough light to survive.

5

Set out your reed diffuser

Place it on your side table or desk. Open your windows for 10 minutes, then come back in. That first walk back into the room is the moment you'll realize the makeover worked.

Honest Tips Before You Shop

Watch Out

Don't buy everything at once if it stretches your budget. The lamp and bedding come first — highest visual impact for the lowest cost. The rest can follow over a few weeks.

  • Check reviews before buying. Especially for bedding — the feel matters and product photos can be misleading.
  • Stick to neutral colors. White, cream, beige, sage, or dusty blue for your first setup. Add color later through smaller, cheaper accessories.
  • Don't hang anything on walls yet. Wall decor is harder to undo and easy to get wrong. Get the basics right first.
  • Shop during Prime Day or Black Friday. These prices can drop significantly during Amazon sale events.
  • Measure before you order. Especially the side table — make sure it fits next to your bed without blocking your path.

What to Add Next

Once you've done this makeover, your room will already feel completely different. Here's what to consider adding next without blowing your budget:

  • A small area rug under the bed ($30–60) — adds warmth and defines the space
  • Floating shelves above your desk ($20–40) — storage and display in one
  • Blackout curtains ($25–50) — game-changing for sleep quality
  • A full-length mirror ($40–80) — makes small rooms look and feel bigger instantly

But seriously? Start with the $170 first. You might be surprised how much you don't need the extras once the basics are done right.

Final Thought

Your bedroom doesn't have to be perfect. It just has to feel like yours. Start small, be intentional, and build from there. You've got this. 🏠
